Early Life & Education

Explore Sam Altman's formative years and educational journey that shaped his path to becoming a tech visionary.

Samuel Harris Altman was born on April 22, 1985, in Chicago, Illinois, and grew up in St. Louis, Missouri. He is the eldest of four siblings in a Jewish family. His mother was a dermatologist, and his father worked in real estate.

Altman's fascination with technology began early. At eight years old, he received his first computer, an Apple Macintosh, and quickly began learning to code. This early exposure ignited a passion that would shape his future career. Discovering AOL chat rooms helped him navigate the challenges of growing up gay in the Midwest during the early 2000s.

He attended John Burroughs School, a private school in Ladue, Missouri, where he openly declared his sexual orientation during a school assembly. He later enrolled at Stanford University to study computer science. However, he dropped out after two years in 2005 to co-found Loopt, a mobile social networking service. Loopt was later acquired in 2012 for $43.4 million, marking an early success in Altman's entrepreneurial journey. Beyond Loopt, he has invested in various technology startups and nuclear energy companies, demonstrating a diverse range of interests.
